The Revenue Waterfall answers: **when will the revenue we've invoiced actually be earned?** It takes amounts you've billed and lays out, period by period, how they release into recognized revenue. It's the clearest view of how a single annual invoice turns into twelve months of revenue.

## When to use it

- Forecasting recognized revenue from contracts you've already invoiced.
- Explaining to finance why this month's recognized revenue differs from this month's billings.
- Auditing how a specific invoice spreads across periods.

## Who uses it

Finance and RevOps doing revenue forecasting and audit.

## Main fields

| Field | What it means | How to use it |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Source | The invoice or subscription the revenue comes from. | Trace a row back to what was billed. |
| Amount invoiced | The total pre-tax amount being released over time. | The starting point of the waterfall. |
| Per-period recognition | How much of that amount is recognized in each period. | Read across to see the release schedule. |
| Recognized to date | The portion earned through the current period. | Where the contract stands now. |
| Remaining | What's still to be recognized in future periods. | Future revenue already locked in. |

## How to read it

Each row is a source of revenue. Reading left to right across the period columns shows how that amount is recognized over time. The columns add up to the full pre-tax amount invoiced.

## Example

A \$1,200 annual subscription invoiced in advance on January 1, released monthly across the year (each month's figure is the sum of that month's daily recognition).

| Source | Amount invoiced | Jan | Feb | Mar | ... | Dec | Remaining at Jan 31 |
|--------|-----------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|---------------------|
| Annual subscription | \$1,200.00 | \$101.92 | \$92.05 | \$101.92 | ... | \$101.92 | \$1,098.08 |

January and March each have 31 days, February 28, so their monthly figures differ slightly even though the daily rate is constant. The row sums to \$1,200.

## Relationship to other reports

- The current-period column matches the [Recognized Revenue report](/guide/revenue-recognition/reports/recognized-revenue).
- The remaining amount matches the [Deferred Revenue report](/guide/revenue-recognition/reports/deferred-revenue) for advance-billed sources.
- Line-level detail is in the [Journal Entry Details](/guide/revenue-recognition/reports/journal-entry-details).

## Limitations to keep in mind

- The waterfall reflects what's been invoiced and recognized. It isn't a forecast of future bookings.
- Figures are pre-tax.
- Contra-revenue events (credit notes, voids) adjust the schedule in the period they occur.
